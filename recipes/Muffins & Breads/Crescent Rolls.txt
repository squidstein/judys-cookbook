# Crescent Rolls

From Toronto Star, August 16, 1966

INGREDIENTS

- 1 pkg yeast
- ¼ c. lukewarm water
- ¾ c. milk
- ½ c. butter
- 4 tbsp. sugar
- 1 tsp. salt
- 3 eggs, beaten
- 4 ½ c. sifted all-purpose flour

INSTRUCTIONS

Soften the yeast in the lukewarm water with 1 tsp. of the sugar and leave for 10 minutes.  Scald the milk and stir in the butter, remaining sugar and salt.  Cool to lukewarm.  Stir in the yeast, eggs and flour.  Knead until a smooth dough is formed.  Place the dough in a greased bowl and cover.  Allow to rise until doubled in bulk.

Punch down the dough and divide into thirds and roll each piece into a 10-inch circle.  Cut the circle into 12 wedge-shaped pieces.  Roll each piece from the wide end down to the point.  Place on a greased baking sheet and pull edges inward to form a crescent.  Cover and let rise until doubled in bulk.  Brush with melted butter.

Bake in a 400-degree oven for 15 minutes.  Makes 36 rolls.

These are the famous crescent rolls that Mom made for Stan’s Bar Mitzvah – How many you ask?  200!!
