# French Bread

From Toronto Star, August 16, 1966

INGREDIENTS

- ½ c. milk
- 1 c. boiling water
- 1 pkg yeast
- ¼ c. lukewarm water
- 1 ½ tbsp. melted shortening
- 1 tbsp. sugar
- 4 c. sifted all-purpose flour
- 2 tsp. salt
- 2 tsp. sugar
- 1 egg white, beaten until fluffy
- 1 tbsp. cold water

INSTRUCTIONS

Scald the milk, and then add the cup of boiling water.  While this liquid cools to lukewarm, dissolve the yeast in the ¼ cup of lukewarm water with 1 tsp. of the sugar.  Leave for 10 minutes.  Add the yeast to the lukewarm milk and water along with the remainder of the 1 tbsp. of sugar and melted shortening.

In a large mixing bowl, measure 4 cups of the flour, the salt and the 2 tsp. sugar.  Make a well and add the liquid ingredients.  Stir very thoroughly but do not knead this mixture.  Cover with a damp cloth and leave in a warm place for two hours.

Punch down the dough and divide in half.  Place the halves on a lightly floured board and with the fingers, press into rectangles.   Roll the rectangles slightly to make them even, then roll the dough like a jellyroll, gently pulling the ends to make a long, thin form.

Place the loaves on a greased baking sheet.  Cut ¼ inch deep slits across the top and set in a warm place to rise.

Bake in a pre-heated 400-degree oven for 15 minutes, over a pie plate filled with water (this pie plate creates the necessary moisture for the bread).  Reduce heat to 350 and bake for another 25 minutes.

Brush the loaves with a mixture of egg white and water and return to the oven for a further five minutes.
